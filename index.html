Earth’s Water Cycle

MS-ESS2-4

Water on Earth is incredibly old. The water in your glass today may have once rained down on an ancient volcano, been frozen for thousands of years in a glacier, or even been slurped out of a puddle by a thirsty dinosaur!

How is that possible?

Water on Earth doesn’t disappear—it’s always on the move. It travels through a continuous journey called the water cycle, powered by energy from the Sun.

In this article, you’ll explore how water moves through the world, and build a model of the water cycle along the way.
